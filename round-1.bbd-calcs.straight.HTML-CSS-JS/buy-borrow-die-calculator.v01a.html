How This Calculator Works

Each year, your assets grow by the Investment Growth %, your existing loan accrues interest, and you take a new borrowing draw that scales with inflation to preserve real spending power. The tool caps total year-end leverage (loan ÷ assets) at your Max Leverage %. If the cap would be exceeded, borrowing is automatically reduced for that year.

Strategy Context

The classic Buy-Borrow-Die approach works as follows:

  • Buy assets that appreciate over time (stocks, real estate, etc.)
  • Borrow against them for spending — borrowing proceeds are not taxed as income
  • Die with appreciated assets that receive a stepped-up basis (IRC §1014), potentially eliminating capital gains tax for heirs

Key Risks

  • Margin calls: If markets fall, lenders may require additional collateral
  • Interest rate risk: Rising rates increase borrowing costs
  • Policy risk: Tax rules (including step-up basis) could change
  • Concentration risk: Works best with diversified, liquid portfolios
